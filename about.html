What is Power Consumption Analysis?

Power Consumption Analysis (PCA) is a system that studies household electricity usage patterns, identifies appliances consuming high energy, and provides insights to optimize power usage. By leveraging sub-metering data and predictive modeling, it helps detect peak consumption periods, forecast future usage, and guide users toward energy efficiency.

How it Works

  1. Data Collection: Household power consumption dataset with voltage, current, and sub-meter readings.
  2. Data Cleaning: Handle missing values, scale features, and prepare input data.
  3. Analysis: Explore patterns such as daily/weekly peaks, appliance-level usage, and anomalies.
  4. Prediction: Use PCA + ML models (Random Forest, ARIMA, etc.) to forecast active power demand.
  5. Recommendations: Suggest energy-saving measures based on high-usage appliances.

How to Reduce High Consumption

  • Turn off appliances when not in use, especially heavy consumers like AC, geysers, and washing machines.
  • Switch to energy-efficient appliances (LED bulbs, inverter refrigerators, star-rated devices).
  • Use appliances during non-peak hours to reduce stress on the grid.
  • Monitor kitchen devices (Sub-meter 1), laundry/heating devices (Sub-meter 2), and HVAC (Sub-meter 3) to cut excess load.
  • Regularly track your consumption trends with the PCA dashboard.

Dataset

This project uses the household_power_consumption dataset, which contains:

  • Date & Time — timestamp of measurement
  • Global_active_power — household active power consumption (kW)
  • Global_reactive_power — reactive power (kW)
  • Voltage — average voltage (V)
  • Global_intensity — current intensity (A)
  • Sub_metering_1,2,3 — appliance-level consumption
